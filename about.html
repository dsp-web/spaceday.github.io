About World Space Week

Origins & Dates

World Space Week was declared by the United Nations General Assembly in 1999. The week is held every year from October 4 to October 10 to commemorate the launch of the first artificial Earth satellite, Sputnik 1 (October 4, 1957), and the entry into force of the Outer Space Treaty (October 10, 1967).

Purpose

The main goals are to:

  • Promote international cooperation in space outreach and education.
  • Inspire students and the public about space sciences and space technology.
  • Highlight the benefits of space services to society (e.g., Earth observation, communications, navigation).

Coordination

The World Space Week Association (WSWA) coordinates events worldwide with support from the United Nations Office for Outer Space Affairs (UNOOSA).

How to participate

Organizers register events on the World Space Week website. Typical participants: schools, universities, museums, space agencies, NGOs, and amateur astronomy clubs.

Key Milestones

  1. 1957 — Sputnik 1 launch (first artificial satellite).
  2. 1961 — Yuri Gagarin becomes the first human in space.
  3. 1969 — Apollo 11 Moon landing.
  4. 1967 — Outer Space Treaty enters into force.
  5. 1999 — UN declares World Space Week (October 4–10).

Sources: UNOOSA, World Space Week Association, major space agencies.
